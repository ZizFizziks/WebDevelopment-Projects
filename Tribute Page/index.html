Jimi Hendrix

The man who set the tone

  • 1942: November 27, 1942 - Jimi Hendrix is born in Seattle, Washington, as Johnny Allen Hendrix.
  • 1958: Jimi Hendrix acquires his first acoustic guitar.
  • 1961: Hendrix joins the United States Army but is honorably discharged in 1962.
  • 1963: Hendrix forms his first band, The King Kasuals, and begins playing in various clubs.
  • 1966: Jimi Hendrix moves to London, where he forms the Jimi Hendrix Experience with bassist Noel Redding and drummer Mitch Mitchell.
  • 1967: "Are You Experienced," the debut album of the Jimi Hendrix Experience, is released and becomes a major success.
  • 1968: The album "Axis: Bold as Love" is released.
  • 1969: Jimi Hendrix performs at the Monterey Pop Festival, gaining fame for his guitar antics.
  • 1969: Hendrix performs at the Woodstock Music and Arts Festival.
  • 1969: "Electric Ladyland," the third album by the Experience, is released.
  • 1970: Hendrix forms the Band of Gypsys with Buddy Miles and Billy Cox.
  • 1970: "Band of Gypsys," a live album, is released.
  • 1970: Tragically, on September 18, 1970, Jimi Hendrix passes away in London at the age of 27.
